Better Habits Battle Royale

Gamifying the process of changing habitual behaviour to add enjoyment and adventure to the long process

Problem Space

Humans are a habitual species – our day to day lives and the involved activities unfold based on previously constructed routines that, given time, become second nature. Everything from when you exercise to when and what you eat is guided by repeated behaviours wired deep in the neurological pathways of our brains. Because of this, the rewiring of these pathways can be very difficult. Creating or changing a new habit can take anywhere from three to six months, an arduous process that discourages many people and is the cause of many unachieved goals. FoDoJoHo’s project goal is to create an application that helps to make this Long term behavioural changes seem shorter and more enjoyable than it usually is.

Concept

Better Habits Battle Royale (BHBR) is a social mobile computing application that aims to help users change undesired habitual behaviours in an interesting, exciting way. BHBR takes the form of a mobile application that will pit users with similar habits against each other to gamify the process of changing a habit by adding the element of friendly competition. Similar to other games in the battle royale genre, BHBR will have a lobby system that allows users to join battles based on their self-identified habit that they want to change – these games will run for a certain amount of time or until all other players have bowed out. Examples of lobbies could range from ‘not biting my nails’ to ‘trying to walk my dog everyday’. Each game is hoped to help rewire the neurological pathways for that habit piece by piece in a way that is actually enjoyable for participants.

BHBR also aims to create a social environment of ‘accountable anonymity’ where users identified only by a unique username will be able to share their habit with others in the same position while competing to create a better situation for all. Key user research insights show that the majority of people would be more comfortable sharing their bad habits with strangers – BHBR leverages this insight by allowing users to play anonymously. It is hoped the users will be able to make friends with strangers around the world in an effort to become better habitual humans.
